Fractals

Fractals are one of mathematics more beautiful aspects and they appear everywhere in nature as well from the bark on a rough tree to the structure of it's branches and it's leaves, in the way clouds form and dirt cracks due to drought, how snowflakes form like fractals and many other naturally occuring instances. It was Bernoit Mandelbrot who first discovered the fractals and later a perticularly famous fractal, namely the Mandelbrot set, has been named after him to honor discovery.

Fractals are also useful in calculating a sort of roughness, this being useful in dealing with the structure of coastlines, for example in the case that potential ships were to sail alongside it, it would be smart to first figure out if that is a good idea due to potential shipwreckage.
